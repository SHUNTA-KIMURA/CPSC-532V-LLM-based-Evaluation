Lionel Andrés "Leo" Messi (born 24 June 1987) is an Argentine professional footballer, captaining both Major League Soccer club Inter Miami and the Argentina national team. Widely regarded as one of history's greatest, Messi has won a record eight Ballon d'Ors, six European Golden Shoes, and eight FIFA World's Best Player awards. In 2025, he was named the All Time Men's World Best Player by the IFFHS.

A prolific goalscorer and creative playmaker, Messi holds records for most goals in a calendar year (91), most goals for a single club (672 for Barcelona), and most goals in La Liga (474). He has the most assists in international football (61) and the highest total goal contributions in the sport's history, with over 890 senior career goals and over 400 assists. At Barcelona, he won a club-record 34 trophies, including ten La Liga titles and four Champions Leagues. He led Inter Miami to their first MLS Cup in 2025, winning back-to-back league MVP awards in 2024 and 2025, and broke MLS records for assists and goal contributions in a single game.

Internationally, Messi captained Argentina to victory in the 2021 Copa América and the 2022 FIFA World Cup, cementing his legacy by winning every top-tier trophy at both club and country levels. Off the field, he has been a UNICEF Goodwill Ambassador since 2010, invested in Argentine youth football, and donated shirts to secure COVID-19 vaccines for South American players. A docuseries, 'Messi's World Cup: The Rise of a Legend,' aired in 2024, and he launched his Más+ sports drink brand in June 2024.
